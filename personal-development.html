What I've learnt

Upon reading the book "the twelve pillars" I've come to learn alot of valuable lessons but two of those lessons stood to to me the most. The first valuable lesson I've from the twelve pillars book is to think of your relationships as tilling a garden. Once a flower blooms it will need water and sun to keep it from wilting. Treat it with care and it will flourish, neglect them and it will surely wither and fade away. Too often do I neglect my relationships, my friends, my family and my significant other, I would very much like to blame it on my introvertedness and my inclination to be alone and have time by myself but I've been realising that in doing that I'm involuntarily pushing people away and out of my life. This realization has really opened my eyes to my ways and I will try and use it to be a better person towards those in my life so they can bloom gracefully.

Have a goal to strive towards, this will keep you grounded and steadfast in the path ahead, most likely without one you'll remain direction less and take life as it hits you, some people can live like this but I choose not to, my goal is to become a software engineer and I will accomplish it by any means necessary that's part of the reason why I'm doing this degree in the first place, to make that dream a reality and be able to say in the end "I stood true to my goals and say it through to the very end, I did it". The book also talks about writing down your goals to bring them into reality and I've been doing something quite similar to that albeit to a smaller scale.

I have a list of 100 things that I want to do before I graduate college and by writing them down I am bringing them into reality and holding myself accountable to those goals, it will not affect me if I don't achieve them but it's within the sentiment that I tried that gives me solace. Another quote from the book that embodies this is "make a million dollars, not for the million dollars but for what you will have become to make a million dollars". With this way of thinking I think it shows what I really want to become at the end of all of this, a better person, a stronger person who looks out for the weak and for himself as well . To become what I envision I have to remain true to my goals and ambition, they are my guide. I am where I am currently at but that does not mean I need to stay there
